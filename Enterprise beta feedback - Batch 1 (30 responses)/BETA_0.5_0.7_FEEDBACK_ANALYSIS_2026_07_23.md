# AXXESS Beta 0.5 and Beta 0.7 Feedback Analysis

Date: 23 July 2026
Product: AXXESS by Triaxis Ventures
Feedback sources: Beta user product feedback survey, NPS report, enterprise beta feedback reports, dashboard export, and related PDF/PPTX artifacts.

## 1. Executive Summary

AXXESS shows unusually strong early product validation across both beta 0.5 and beta 0.7 feedback sets. The combined short-form survey shows an NPS of **82.61**, with **20 of 23 respondents giving a 10/10 recommendation score**. The detailed enterprise feedback set shows consistently high ratings across product impression, enterprise readiness, AI capability, governance, usability, and adoption intent.

The strongest market signal is that users are not simply reacting positively to the interface. They are identifying AXXESS as an **AI-native institutional operating platform**, with highest perceived value in:

- AI Workspace and model routing
- Enterprise workflow and task management
- Human-in-the-loop review
- Documents, knowledge, and RAG
- Governance and policy controls

The strongest gaps are also clear:

- Feature completeness
- Integrations
- AI output quality
- Performance/reliability
- Too many screens or workflow complexity
- Mobile experience

This means AXXESS has moved beyond "interesting prototype" feedback and into a more serious enterprise-beta pattern: users understand the product category, value the operating-system thesis, and are asking for deeper functionality, not a different product.

## 3. Important Data Caveat

The provided PDF/PPTX exports are report-level and chart-level exports, not raw row-level response exports. Therefore:

- Aggregate results can be analyzed reliably.
- File-level duplicate exports can be deduped.
- The user-provided response splits define beta 0.5 and beta 0.7 cohorts.
- Exact beta 0.5 versus beta 0.7 numeric deltas cannot be calculated from the exported charts alone unless raw CSV/XLS response data is provided.

This analysis therefore separates:

1. What the aggregate feedback proves.
2. What can be reasonably inferred about beta progression.
3. What requires raw response data for exact cohort comparison.

## 4. Short Product Feedback Survey Findings

Total responses: **23**

### 4.1 Recommendation / NPS

Question: How likely are you to recommend AXXESS to a colleague, organization, or professional contact?

Result:

- NPS: **82.61**
- Average recommendation score: **9.61 / 10**
- 10/10 responses: **20**
- 8/10 responses: **1**
- 7/10 responses: **1**
- 6/10 responses: **1**

Interpretation:

This is an exceptionally strong recommendation signal for an early enterprise beta. A score above 80 suggests that users are not merely tolerant of the product; many are enthusiastic enough to recommend it.

The presence of only one clear detractor-level score also suggests that dissatisfaction is concentrated in fixable execution gaps rather than rejection of the product thesis.

### 4.2 Disappointment If AXXESS Were No Longer Available

Question: How disappointed would you be if AXXESS were no longer available?

Result:

- Average score: **8.35 / 10**

This is a strong retention-style signal. Users are indicating that AXXESS would matter if removed, which is more meaningful than surface-level liking.

## 6. Beta 0.5 vs Beta 0.7 Interpretation

### Known cohort split

Short form:

- Beta 0.5: responses 1-15
- Beta 0.7: responses 16-23

Detailed form:

- Beta 0.5: responses 1-7
- Beta 0.7: responses 8-10

### What can be said confidently

The combined beta 0.5 and beta 0.7 dataset shows:

- Very high recommendation intent.
- Strong AI-native perception.
- High enterprise-readiness perception.
- Strong trust and governance scores.
- Strong interest in adoption, pilots, and future participation.
- Clear demand for integrations, workflow completion, mobile, OCR, and improved AI reliability.

### What cannot be calculated from current artifacts

Because the exports are chart-level reports rather than row-level response data, the exact beta 0.5 to beta 0.7 numeric delta cannot be calculated with statistical confidence.

For example, we cannot precisely calculate from the PDFs alone:

- Beta 0.5 NPS versus beta 0.7 NPS
- Beta 0.5 AI-native average versus beta 0.7 AI-native average
- Beta 0.5 adoption intent versus beta 0.7 adoption intent
- Whether the lower scores were concentrated in beta 0.5 or beta 0.7

### Reasonable product interpretation

Even without exact cohort math, the aggregate beta 0.7-inclusive dataset suggests that later feedback did not dilute the product signal. The final combined results remain extremely strong after the beta 0.7 responses were added.

That is meaningful because beta 0.7 responses expanded the dataset while preserving:

- NPS above 80
- AI-native perception above 8.7/10
- Enterprise product ratings above 9/10
- Enterprise readiness above 9/10
- Strong willingness to trust the platform with sensitive organizational data

The most useful next step is to collect or export raw response data and compute a true cohort delta table.

## 10. Product Roadmap Implications

The beta feedback points to the following priority order.

### Priority 1: Complete live workflows

Users want the product to move from impressive platform to executable system. The golden path should become fully reliable:

Sign up -> create tenant -> upload/import knowledge -> ask AXXESS -> review cited answer -> approve action -> create task/project/approval -> see dashboard/audit/timeline update.

### Priority 2: Deepen integrations

The strongest integration requests are:

- Email
- Calendar
- WhatsApp
- Mobile
- OCR/document processing

Email and calendar should remain first because they unlock institutional workflow capture.

### Priority 3: Improve AI reliability and trust

Required improvements:

- Better citations
- Better source display
- Confidence indicators
- Review/approval queue
- Tenant-aware retrieval
- Permission-aware retrieval
- Audit logs for AI answers
- Better output quality evaluation

### Priority 4: Simplify workflow complexity

The "too many screens" signal means AXXESS should maintain its architecture but improve navigation, guided flows, and contextual CTAs.

This does not imply redesigning the UI from scratch. It implies stronger journey design.

### Priority 5: Mobile and field readiness

Mobile was repeatedly requested. For public-sector, healthcare, NGO, and education contexts, field usability may become a key adoption factor.

### Priority 6: Buyer-grade trust package

Prepare:

- Security documentation
- Data handling documentation
- RLS / tenant-isolation explanation
- Audit logging explanation
- AI governance explanation
- Compliance posture
- Deployment architecture
- Enterprise onboarding guide
